# Inventory Rationalisation Review
*Southern Railway Signalling & Telecom Spare Parts — 07 June 2026*

## Executive Summary

A review of stock movement identifies 327 items for rationalisation (slow moving, holding can be reduced) and 190 items for disposal (dead stock), while 348 items are healthy and should be retained at current levels. Together the rationalisation and disposal candidates account for Rs 24.05 crore of inventory value. Acting on them reduces the number of lines carried, frees depot space, and releases working capital that is presently idle. None of this affects fast-moving or critical items, which are retained. Management is asked to approve a phased rationalisation drive, taking the highest-value slow-moving and dead items first, so that the reduction in holding is achieved without any risk to service.

## Rationalization Candidates

- **Rationalise (reduce holding):** 327 items, Rs 16.42 crore

> **Observation.** 327 slow-moving items worth Rs 16.42 crore are carried at levels above what their movement justifies.
>
> **Business Implication.** Holding more than is needed of slow movers ties up capital and space that active items could use.
>
> **Recommended Action.** Reduce reorder levels for these items and avoid fresh purchases until stock draws down.


## Disposal Candidates

- **Dispose (dead stock):** 190 items, Rs 7.63 crore

> **Observation.** 190 dead-stock items worth Rs 7.63 crore are not moving at all.
>
> **Business Implication.** These will not be issued in the foreseeable future and only consume space and capital.
>
> **Recommended Action.** Route these items to the dead-stock board for transfer, reuse or disposal.


## Savings Opportunity

- **Total value affected:** Rs 24.05 crore
- **Items retained (no change):** 348

> **Observation.** Rationalisation and disposal together address Rs 24.05 crore of holding.
>
> **Business Implication.** This is working capital that can be progressively released and redirected to critical procurement.
>
> **Recommended Action.** Approve a phased drive and re-deploy the released capital towards the immediate procurement list.

## Key Findings

- 327 items to rationalise (Rs 16.42 crore).
- 190 items to dispose (Rs 7.63 crore).
- 348 items retained at current levels.
- Total value affected: Rs 24.05 crore.


## Business Implications

- Carrying lines beyond their movement need inflates holding cost and crowds depots.
- Released working capital can be redirected to fund critical procurement.
- Fast-moving and critical items are untouched, so service is not affected.


## Recommended Actions

1. **Approve a phased rationalisation drive** covering the 517 slow-moving and dead items.
2. **Lower reorder levels** on the 327 rationalisation candidates.
3. **Dispose / transfer** the 190 dead-stock items.
4. **Redirect released capital** of up to Rs 24.05 crore towards critical procurement.
