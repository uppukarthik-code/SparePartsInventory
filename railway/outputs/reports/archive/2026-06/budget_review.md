# Procurement Budget Review
*Southern Railway Signalling & Telecom Spare Parts — 07 June 2026*

## Executive Summary

The total procurement requirement is Rs 47.31 crore. Four budget scenarios were tested to see how much of that requirement each would fund and how many critical items it would cover. Even the largest scenario (Rs 5.00 crore) funds only 10.6% of the requirement, leaving Rs 42.31 crore unfunded. Because the requirement is so much larger than any single sanction, each additional rupee buys broadly similar coverage, and no one year can close the gap. The practical course is a multi-year programme that always funds the most critical items first. The recommended first-year tranche is **Rs 5 Crore**, which secures the widest criticality coverage available in this set (14.0%).

## Scenario Comparison

| Scenario | Budget | Items Funded | Procurement Coverage | Criticality Coverage | Remaining Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| Rs 1 Crore | Rs 1.00 crore | 6 | 2.1% | 2.8% | Rs 46.31 crore |
| Rs 2 Crore | Rs 2.00 crore | 8 | 4.2% | 5.5% | Rs 45.31 crore |
| Rs 5 Crore | Rs 5.00 crore | 6 | 10.6% | 14.0% | Rs 42.31 crore |


## Procurement Coverage

> **Observation.** The largest scenario (Rs 5.00 crore) covers 10.6% of the Rs 47.31 crore requirement.
>
> **Business Implication.** A single year's budget cannot materially reduce the procurement backlog.
>
> **Recommended Action.** Plan procurement as a multi-year programme rather than expecting one sanction to clear it.


## Criticality Coverage

> **Observation.** The recommended tranche, Rs 5 Crore, reaches 14.0% criticality coverage — the highest in the set.
>
> **Business Implication.** When funds are scarce, covering the most critical items matters more than the rupee value funded.
>
> **Recommended Action.** Always allocate the budget to safety (S1) and vital (S2) items before lower-criticality stock.


## Remaining Gaps

- **Unfunded at largest scenario:** Rs 42.31 crore

> **Observation.** Rs 42.31 crore of requirement remains unfunded even at the top scenario.
>
> **Business Implication.** Critical items left unfunded each year remain a standing service risk.
>
> **Recommended Action.** Carry the unfunded critical items forward as the first call on the next year's budget.


## Diminishing Returns

Across these scenarios each additional rupee returns broadly the same coverage (about 2.11% of the requirement per crore), because the requirement far exceeds every budget tested. The constraint is the size of the backlog, not diminishing efficiency, which reinforces the case for a sustained multi-year plan.


## Key Findings

- Procurement requirement Rs 47.31 crore.
- Largest scenario funds 10.6%; Rs 42.31 crore remains unfunded.
- Recommended first tranche: Rs 5 Crore (14.0% criticality coverage).


## Business Implications

- No single-year budget closes the procurement gap.
- Funding out of criticality order would leave the most important items short.


## Recommended Scenario

Adopt **Rs 5 Crore** (Rs 5.00 crore) as the first-year tranche — it secures the widest criticality coverage in the set (14.0%) — and commit to a multi-year programme to fund the balance of Rs 47.31 crore in criticality order.
